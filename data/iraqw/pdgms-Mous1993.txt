NOTE:
Mous1993 pp.123-154. Mous calls this section “The verbs ‘to be’” rather than “selector”. In any case nearly every sentence in Iraqw contains a selector/”verb to be” whether the complement is nominal or verbal. [Ex: tlee’use a Manda [potters COP Bantu] ‘Potters are Bantu’; aníng a goo’—ïim [1.SG BE write-DUR:1.SG] ‘I am writing’]
