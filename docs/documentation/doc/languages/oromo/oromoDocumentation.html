General Information

Location-Speakers

Varieties and subdialects of Oromo are spoken by millions of individuals over much of central and southern Ethiopia . The variety treated here is characteristic of the west-central region, specifically Wallagga. Other more-or-less distinct varieties are the Eastern , Borana-Arsi-Guji, and Orma (spoken in Kenya).

Bibliographic Source

Verb Properties

General Characterization: In the affirmative main clause, Oromo makes the usual simple tense distinctions. The non-compound categories present/past, affirmative/negative, and main/subordinate interact in the following complex way, illustrated in the table below. In the present affirmative, the main clause PNG suffixes have a characteristic -a vocalization as opposed to a -u vocalization in the subordinate. In the present negative, the mainClause forms have the same -u PNG series as the subordinate main; the present negative subordinate has an invariant -ne PNG suffix. In the past tense, there is no distinction between main and subordinate forms. The affirmative past tense has an -e PNG vocalization, while the negative main and subordinate now has the invariant -ne.The following table illustrates the distribution of PNG+tense markers, where the PNG sequence is: 1sg, 2sg, 3msg, 3fsg, 1pl, 2pl, 3pl (i.e., the Cushitic-Omotic Pattern):

In the compound tenses Oromo makes characteristic distinctions of tense and aspect (perfectivity and progressivity) as described in documentation guide, and indicated here, as usual. by approximate glosses.

  • polarity

    1. affirmative: if "emphatic" or foregrounded, can be preceeded by proclitic (h)ín- in present tense.

    2. negative: takes negative proclitic ((h)i)n-, plus special negative tenses, as described above. When reduced to simple segment -n-, the particle can be encliticized to preceeding word.

  • theme

    1. base

    2. causative

    3. middle

    4. passive

  • tam

    • simple

      1. present

      2. past

      3. jussive

      4. imperative

    • compound

      1. perfect. Structure:V[past]+jir-[pres] Gloss:"he has gone, . . ."

      2. pluperfect. Structure:V[past]+tur-[pst] Gloss: "he had gone, . . ."

      3. presentProgressive. Structure:V[participle]+jir-[pres] Gloss: "he is going, . . ."

      4. pastProgressive. Structure:V[participle]+tur-[past] Gloss: "he was going, . . ."

      5. pluperfectProgressive. Structure:V[participle]+jir-[pres]+tur-[past] Gloss: "he had been going, . . ."

      6. futureDefinite. Structure:V[infiitive]+f+jir-[pres] Gloss: "he will go, . . ."

      7. futureIndefinite. Structure:hín+V[pres]+ta'a[3msg-invariable] Gloss: "he may go, . . ."

      8. futurePerfect. Structure:V[infinitive]+f+tur-[past] Gloss: "he will have gone, . . ."

      9. conditional. Structure:hín+V[pres]+tur-[past] Gloss: "if he goes, . . ."

  • special

    • BE: Oromo has an uninflected enclitic for the simple positive (-ɗa) and negative (-miti) copula. Otherwise for BE it uses suppletively the following verbs:

      1. tur-: predication past-tense; existence, past

      2. ta-: predication, non-past/non-present; occurrence, all tenses

      3. jir-: existence, present

  • nonFinite

    1. infinitive

    2. agent

    3. gerund
